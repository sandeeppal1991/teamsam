In March 2018,
Sacramento police received a call about someone breaking windows in the neighbourhood.

When the helicopter spotted a man in his backyard, officers on the ground wasted no time in confronting the black man and firing 8 shots at him, killing him instantly.


They suspected he was carrying a weapon.

TURNS OUT, ALL HE HAD ON HIM WAS HIS PHONE.

This incident prompted us to explore the behavior of police and what factors, if any, might influence the actions of police in various circumstances.

                                      Race not a factor?

                                      Whites, African-Americans & Hispanics killed from 2013 to 2017. Since the beginning of the ‘Black Lives Matter’ movement in 2013, we can see that the number of African-Americans killed has not increased significantly.

                                      Fleeing doesn't help

                                      In general, there has been an increase in the number of people killed, regardless of whether they were fleeing or not. It would be interesting to see how people who don't flee react in such situations.
